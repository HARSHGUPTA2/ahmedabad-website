Ahmedabad: UNESCO World Heritage City & Cultural Capital of Gujarat

Ahmedabad , Gujarat's largest city and India's first UNESCO World Heritage City, offers an extraordinary blend of ancient heritage and modern dynamism. Founded in 1411 by Sultan Ahmed Shah, this magnificent city serves as the perfect gateway to explore Gujarat's rich cultural tapestry, from historic stepwells and magnificent mosques to vibrant festivals and authentic Gujarati cuisine.


Ahmedabad: Must Visit Places And Heritage

Sabarmati Ashram
Location: Gandhi Smarak Sangrahalaya, Ahmedabad
Highlight: Historic home of Mahatma Gandhi, peaceful riverfront, museum, and library.
Adalaj Stepwell
Location: Adalaj, Gandhinagar-Ahmedabad Road
Highlight: 15th-century intricately carved stepwell, unique Indo-Islamic architecture, cool interiors.
Kankaria Lake
Location: Maninagar, Ahmedabad
Highlight: Scenic lakefront, zoo, toy train, amusement park, balloon ride, and evening light shows.
Sidi Saiyyed Mosque
Location: Near Lal Darwaja, Ahmedabad
Highlight: Famous for its iconic stone latticework windows (jalis), symbol of Ahmedabad.
Jama Masjid
Location: Manek Chowk, Ahmedabad
Highlight: 15th-century grand mosque, yellow sandstone, beautiful domes and minarets.
Calico Museum of Textiles
Location: Shahibaug, Ahmedabad
Highlight: Renowned textile museum, rare fabrics, costumes, and handicrafts from across India.
Science City
Location: Sola, Ahmedabad
Highlight: Interactive science museum, IMAX theatre, robotics gallery, aquarium, and planetarium.
